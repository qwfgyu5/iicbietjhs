Introduction

RoboRace is a fun and exciting event competition where Youth will think, innovate, ideate and design impactful solutions to face real-world challenges. It offers easy- going free Robotic training to youngsters. The challenge is to build your own robot either wired or wireless within the specified dimensions in order to achieve the maximum speed to beat other robots on the given track and reach the finishing line in minimum time. It requires a mix of knowledge, patience and focus in order to win.

Flow of Event


Introduction:
The Participants will be briefed about the event and rules. They will also be given some basic ideas about track and about what components they can use and about point systems. If required they will also be trained about Robo making and designing skills.

Team Building:
The Participants will form a team of minimum 2 and maximum 4 and register for the event. They also need to prepare a document about components used.

Track Walkthrough:
The Participants will be shown the full Race track on which they need to race. They will not be allowed to test their Robo but they can observe the track.

Modification:
After observing the track, they will be given some time to modify their Robo according to track. Mentors will be there to help them.

Race:
Finally it's time to race and compete with others to be the winner. It will be one at a time on the track. Scoring will be done on the basis of our rating system and the decision of jury will be final.

Prize Distribution:
Top 3 Participants who finishes the track in least time will be awarded with prizes.


Who can participate?


Undergraduate college students from any Indian college, with knowledge of basic electronic components like motor and driving skill can participate.


Rules and Regulations


● A team must contain minimum 2 and maximum 4 members. One will be a driver and the rest will be support.
● Teams must have a proper documentation of Robo containing a brief description about parts and working.
● Once fallen off the track, then can continue from the last checkpoint.
● Robo should be made according to the rules and regulations otherwise it will lead to disqualification.
● Make sure you keep your Robo secret otherwise if found copied will result in disqualification.
● The Robot must not damage the arena.
● Each team must be given a name as chosen by its members. Team names must not be connected in any way with the names of companies or registered trademarks or use expressions that incite violence or are discriminatory, obscene, or represent any form of defamation. Failure to comply will result in disqualification.
● Unethical behavior may lead to disqualification.
● Administrator reserves the right in their sole discretion to disqualify any individual or maker it finds to be actually or presenting the appearance of tampering with the entry process or the operation of the event or to be acting in violation of these Official Rules or in a manner that is inappropriate, unsportsmanlike, not in the best interests of this Event, or a violation of any applicable law or regulation.
Scoring:
● Flag points = 100 + 5 x (no. of checkpoints crossed) – 5 x (no. of falls).
● Time limit will be 15 mins.
● Time bonus will be awarded if done before 15 mins.
● +5 if done crossed 5 checkpoints without falling off the track.
● Total Score = Flag points + (15 – time taken) + bonus points.
Undergraduate college students from any Indian college, with knowledge of basic electronic components like motor and driving skill can participate.
Robo Specifications:
● The dimensions of the robot can be between 25cm x 20cm x 15cm (LxBxH) and 30cm x 25cm x20cm (LxBxH).
● The Robo may be wired or wireless.
● The length of the wire (for wired bots) should be about 1.5 to 2.5m and the wire should remain slack during the complete run.
● Maximum weight must not exceed 3 kg.
● Participants should be able to briefly describe the components used and its working.
● If cheating or a ready-made kit is found, such a machine will be disqualified.
