Introduction

We have been using bridges for centuries to cross the obstacles such as valleys, rough terrain or bodies of water. To make the bridge last longer, it has to be made stronger and more durable.
In this event we are going to conduct a competition of constructing a suspension bridge using truss girder of popsicle sticks of standard dimensions and the strength of the bridges willbe examined by applying load to the Centre of the top side of the bridge.


Flow of Event


Introduction:
The participants will be briefed about the details of the event. All the rules and regulations will be stated again for the flawless conduction of the event.

Team Building:
Students will then be allowed to make a team consisting of 4-5 members. .

Working Time:
The working time can be flexible, depending upon the complexity of the Structure designing.

Presentation:
After the completion of working time, the teams will be called for the final presentation of their stable structure.

Testing:
The final submitted structure will be evaluated based on its design and also using weights.

Award Distribution:
The team whose structure withstands the maximum pressure and does not collapse under simulated conditions will be considered winner. The winning team will be awarded certificates, medals and sponsor rewards.


Who can Participate?

Students from any school (9th to 12th standard) and any student from any recognized institution or college can participate in this event.

Structural Constraints

1. Bridge length must be in range (min 600 mm – max 800 mm)
2. Bridge width must be in range (min 100mm – max 150 mm)
3. Truss girder height must not be greater than 150 mm.
4. Each participant, or team will work with maximum 300 popsicle sticks (standard size 4.5 inch)for truss girder(base) and cotton thread (diameter should not be more than 2 mm) for suspension.

Rules and Regulations

1. This is team event. Each team should have 3 to 5 members.
2. The winning bridge is that which holds the largest amount of load.
3. The bridges will be weighted (number of popsicle sticks) before testing. In the case of a tie, the lightest bridge will be declared the winner.
4. Laminating is allowed to make the thin sticks thicker.
5. Bridges will be broken as part of testing.
6. Loads will be applied to the center of the top side of the bridge.
7. The load will be applied till the bridge is completely broken.
8. No need to make the column for foundation of bridge. The bridges will be loaded from the top of truss girder; the loading bar will be placed roughly over the center.
9. The use of plastic glue/ hot glue/glue gun is strictly prohibited.
10. Any paint or any other coating aside from glue is not allowed.
11. Use of prohibited items will lead to immediate disqualification.
12. Write the name of your team members, branch, year or class on a slip attached to your bridge.
13. All purpose, nontoxic glue (note: crazy and industrial glue are not permitted). White glue is the only glue permitted (e.g., fevicol MR, fevicol SH, wooden fevicol).
14. Hot glue (glue gun) is strictly prohibited.
